Strategy #1: Implement Robust Tip Reporting Systems

Why This Matters

The credit is only available on reported tips. Unreported tips = $0 credit. The more tips properly reported, the larger your refund.

Action Steps:

  • Modernize Your POS System: Use a system that automatically tracks and reports tips. Systems like Toast, Square, or Clover make tip reporting seamless.

Strategy #3: Include ALL Tipped Employees

Many restaurants only calculate credits for servers, missing significant amounts from other tipped staff.

Don't Forget These Positions:

  • Bartenders: Often the highest tip earners
  • Bussers: If they receive tip-outs, they qualify
  • Food Runners: Tip pools include them
  • Barbacks: Backend support that shares tips
  • Sommeliers: High-value tips in fine dining
  • Hosts/Hostesses: If they receive any tip share
  • Captains: Senior service staff
  • Delivery Drivers: Tips from deliveries count

Pro Tip: Even employees who only occasionally receive tips should be included. Part-time and seasonal workers count too.

The credit calculation is based on actual hours worked. More documented hours = larger credit potential.

More hours means a larger "minimum wage requirement" in the calculation, which increases your creditable tips. Plus, accurate records prevent IRS questions that could delay your refund.
